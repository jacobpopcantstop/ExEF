# EFI OT Advisor Proposal

## Purpose

This document is a working proposal for bringing an occupational therapy advisor into EFI in a way that is useful, bounded, and mutually beneficial.

The aim is to create real value for both parties:

- EFI gains stronger clinical precision, better scope-of-practice language, and better practitioner-facing materials.
- The advisor gains paid work, visibility if desired, authorship or reviewer credit where appropriate, and a platform for high-quality interdisciplinary influence.

## Why this role matters

EFI is already strong in pedagogy, structure, and product design. The OT advisor role would sharpen the parts of the site where interdisciplinary legitimacy matters most:

- coaching vs therapy boundaries
- referral language
- special-populations phrasing
- school/home implementation detail
- environmental supports and functional performance framing
- practical guidance for families, educators, and professionals

This role should improve the substance of the product, not just add prestige language.

## Proposed role title options

Choose one that matches the real scope.

- Occupational Therapy Advisor
- Clinical Precision Advisor
- Advisor for Functional Performance and Scope-of-Practice Review
- Curriculum Advisor for Applied OT Perspective

Avoid titles that imply licensure delegation, treatment oversight, supervision, or accreditation unless those are actually true.

## Recommended starting structure

Best initial arrangement:

- role: paid advisor / reviewer
- term: 60 to 90 day pilot
- scope: review, revision guidance, and one or two co-created assets
- decision point: renew, expand, or conclude after pilot

This keeps the first phase concrete and low-risk.

## Compensation models

Use one of these depending on the level of involvement.

### Option A: flat review fee

Best for a narrow first engagement.

Example structure:

- flat fee for review of selected pages
- one revision round included
- optional add-on fee for a second pass

Best when:

- the scope is mostly markup and feedback
- you want speed and simplicity

### Option B: advisor retainer

Best for an ongoing but bounded relationship.

Example structure:

- monthly retainer
- fixed number of review hours
- fixed turnaround expectations

Best when:

- EFI expects recurring copy, curriculum, and product review

### Option C: deliverable fee plus revenue share on co-created products

Best for deeper upside alignment.

Example structure:

- fixed fee for advisory review
- revenue share only on products/assets they materially help create

Best when:

- a workshop, guide, module, or specialty track is jointly built

Avoid vague “advisor equity” unless you are both serious about a long-term company-building relationship.

## What EFI can offer

Potential value to the advisor:

- direct consulting compensation
- named advisor credit, if desired
- author or reviewer attribution on specific assets
- backlinks/profile visibility on EFI
- distribution to EFI’s audience
- co-created workshops or product revenue
- a platform to shape how EF coaching talks about function, environment, and referral boundaries

## What EFI should ask for

Keep this exact and limited.

- review of specific pages and tools
- recommended wording changes
- boundary/risk notes
- public-use permission for agreed advisor language
- optional participation in one co-created asset

Do not assume:

- their name can be used broadly across the site
- they are endorsing every page
- they are supervising the program clinically
- they are taking legal responsibility for your content

## Suggested public language

Only use language the advisor explicitly approves.

Safe examples:

- `Reviewed for scope-of-practice clarity by an occupational therapy advisor.`
- `Special-populations and referral-boundary language reviewed by an occupational therapy advisor.`
- `Selected curriculum and public-facing materials reviewed for functional performance and scope-of-practice precision.`

Avoid:

- `clinically approved`
- `medically endorsed`
- `supervised by`
- `accredited by`

## Recommended pilot terms

- term: 60 days
- meetings: 1 kickoff + 1 review meeting + 1 closeout meeting
- review scope: 7 to 10 pages
- deliverables:
  - markup/review memo
  - prioritized change list
  - one co-created asset concept or outline
- compensation:
  - flat review fee or small retainer
  - optional separate fee for co-created asset

## Draft offer language

EFI is at a stage where stronger OT-informed review could materially improve the quality and defensibility of the site. I’d like to bring you in as an advisor for a defined pilot, focused on scope-of-practice clarity, special-populations language, referral boundaries, and practical function/environment guidance.

I do not want to use your credentials as decorative credibility. I want the relationship to produce real improvements to the curriculum and public-facing materials, with clear boundaries around what your role does and does not imply.

My ideal starting structure is a paid 60-day pilot with a defined page set, one review memo, and one possible co-created public asset if the fit feels strong.
