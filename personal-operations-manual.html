What This Is

A personal operating manual is a practical “how to work with me” guide. It helps people understand your schedule, communication style, strengths, and limits before confusion starts.

How To Use It

You do not need to answer every question. Start with the checkboxes, then only write out the parts that would genuinely help another person work with you better.

Why It Helps

Clear expectations reduce friction. A good manual makes onboarding, feedback, handoffs, and everyday collaboration easier for everyone around you.

What To Download

When you are done, export a plain-text version for docs or chat, or a polished HTML version for a wiki, onboarding page, or team handbook.

Fastest Good Version

Fill these first if you want a useful manual fast

  • Your schedule and when people should not expect a reply
  • Your best contact method for normal questions versus urgent issues
  • How you prefer feedback and written follow-up
  • What stress looks like for you and what actually helps
  • The topics or work people can reliably come to you for

Source note

This builder follows a classic personal operating manual structure and adapts it into a cleaner, more flexible format for modern teams, clients, and collaborators.

Share It With Your Team

Share It Where Your Team Already Works

  • Turn your manual into a wiki page, shared doc, or internal profile and encourage teammates to complete theirs.
  • Share your manual in a team space so people can read it before working closely with you.
  • Add a link to your manual in your profile, onboarding docs, or team bookmarks.
  • Review it with your manager or team lead during a one-to-one or onboarding huddle.
  • Set a reminder to update it when your role, schedule, or personal constraints change.

Actions

Download Or Share

Use text for copy-paste into docs and chat. Use HTML for a cleaner page you can upload or share as-is.

Good POMs Are

  • Specific enough that a teammate can actually act on what they read.
  • Honest about constraints, not just strengths and polished preferences.
  • Updated over time rather than treated like a one-time personality exercise.
  • Flexible: add or ignore sections based on what matters in your role and environment.
