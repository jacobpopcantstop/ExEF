What Families Usually Bring to the First Call

The issue is rarely raw intelligence. It is the gap between what a student clearly can do and what consistently happens at home, in class, and under stress.

"They're so smart, but..."

Your child aces tests when engaged but "forgets" to turn in homework. Their potential is obvious—so why isn't it showing up?

Tutoring feels like punishment

You've tried tutors, but sitting through more "school after school" creates resistance, not results.

The IEP maze is overwhelming

"Preferential seating" and "extended time" sound helpful—but you're not sure your child is getting what they actually need.

Executive Functioning is the missing piece

It's not about being smarter. It's about planning, prioritizing, and following through—skills that can be trained.

What EFI is solving for Not just better grades, but better initiation, less conflict, clearer systems, and more realistic support around the way a student actually functions.

The Play-Based Neuroplasticity Approach

EFI does not position coaching as more homework help. The practice uses activities that naturally build executive function while staying genuinely engaging.

Why Families Choose EFI Coaching

This practice should feel like a serious local operator with a clear methodology, not a generic coaching page with nicer copy.

Practice Position

EFI sits between academic support, executive functioning coaching, and school-navigation consulting. That combination matters because many families do not need three separate providers; they need one practitioner who can connect the dots.

  • Coaching sessions tied to real school and life load
  • Parent-facing translation around accommodations and meetings
  • Engagement tools chosen for actual executive-function fit

School-System Fluency

The practice is built by an educator who understands IEPs, 504s, and how to help families prepare for school conversations and identify accommodations that actually work instead of default checkbox language.

Students Actually Like Sessions

EFI uses improv, strategy games, and structured play to build buy-in. Sessions are designed to feel active and collaborative, not punitive.

Local, But Professional

EFI is rooted in San Diego and understands local districts, private schools, and regional referral networks without reading like a one-person side project.

Evidence-Based, Not Boring

Every game and activity is anchored in executive function and neuroplasticity research. Fun is not fluff here; it is part of the intervention design.

How to Work Together
