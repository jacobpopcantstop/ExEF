# EFI Interdisciplinary Offer Ladder

**Date:** 2026-03-19  
**Purpose:** Define paid offerings EFI can build with an occupational therapist and a special education teacher involved, including pricing, packaging, positioning, and site integration.

---

## Core Positioning Shift

With both a special education teacher and an occupational therapist involved, EFI can move from a strong executive-function education/coaching brand into a more distinctive **interdisciplinary support institute**.

That creates real commercial value in four areas:
- school-to-home translation
- environment and routine design
- practical support planning for students/families
- practitioner training that is more grounded than generic EF coaching

The key is not vague prestige. The value comes from clearly defined outputs:
- interpretation
- support plans
- implementation tools
- training
- review

---

## What Each Expert Adds

### Special Education Teacher
- classroom implementation realism
- school workflow and accommodation knowledge
- parent-school translation
- IEP/504-adjacent practical guidance
- educator-facing credibility

### Occupational Therapist
- environment and participation framing
- functional routine design
- task analysis
- regulation and sensory-load context
- home/school support realism

### Highest-Value Overlap
- translating executive-function difficulty into actual daily supports
- designing home/school routines that are usable
- distinguishing EF coaching from therapy, accommodations, and referral needs
- helping families and educators know what to do next

## Best Paid Offer Categories

### 1. Parent / Family Review Offers

These are interpretation and support-planning products for families who need clarity, not endless sessions.

#### Offer: School-to-Home Translation Review
- **Who it is for:** parents with a child or teen already struggling at school, at home, or both
- **What it includes:**
- intake form
- upload of school notes, testing summaries, teacher concerns, or parent observations
- async review
- short written interpretation memo
- recommended next supports and questions to ask
- **Best value proposition:** "Translate school language into a usable home and support plan."
- **Suggested pricing:** `195–425`

#### Offer: Family EF Support Blueprint
- **Who it is for:** families who want a more complete plan rather than a single interpretation memo
- **What it includes:**
- intake + one or two EFI tools
- cross-signal summary
- top friction points
- home routine supports
- school collaboration suggestions
- referral flags if appropriate
- one consult call or async follow-up
- **Best value proposition:** "One practical support blueprint across home, school, and daily function."
- **Suggested pricing:** `395–695`

---

### 2. Educator / School Offers

These are implementation products for teachers, learning specialists, tutors, and small school teams.

#### Offer: Classroom EF Systems Audit
- **Who it is for:** individual teachers or specialists
- **What it includes:**
- classroom/routine audit intake
- friction mapping across starts, transitions, organization, and follow-through
- recommended systems and accommodations
- short implementation brief
- **Best value proposition:** "Fix the routines and supports that keep students from using the skills they already have."
- **Suggested pricing:** `295–600`

#### Offer: Team Training or School Workshop
- **Who it is for:** schools, programs, learning centers
- **What it includes:**
- 60–120 minute workshop
- implementation framework
- staff handouts and tools
- optional follow-up Q&A
- **Best value proposition:** "Move EF support from vague philosophy to repeatable classroom practice."
- **Suggested pricing:** `900–2,500` for small teams
- **Longer-term B2B range:** `3,000+` for custom or multi-session work

---

### 3. Student / Learner Support Offers

These work best when positioned as short-term intensives with a defined target, not open-ended coaching.

#### Offer: EF Launch Intensive
- **Who it is for:** students or adults with a clear stuck point like homework starts, transition chaos, planning failure, or overload
- **What it includes:**
- focused intake
- short intervention block
- one or two measurable support experiments
- accountability or review touchpoints
- **Best value proposition:** "A short, practical reset for one high-friction EF problem."
- **Suggested pricing:** `450–900` for lighter async / hybrid
- **Higher-touch version:** `1,200–2,500`

---

### 4. Toolkit / Downloadable Products

These are the lowest-friction paid entry offers and should be designed to lead into higher-ticket reviews or consults.

#### Possible Toolkit Themes
- home launch routine pack
- transition supports pack
- homework startup kit
- classroom environment checklist
- regulation + recovery supports
- family meeting / planning templates
- visual supports and accountability templates

#### Recommended Pricing
- `19–49` for small single-purpose tools
- `59–149` for bundled kits
- `149–299` for practitioner packs

#### Why This Matters
- gives EFI a true low-ticket entry
- builds the email list with buyers, not just free users
- creates clearer product ladders into consults and training

---

### 5. Practitioner Training Offers

This is likely the biggest long-term differentiator if EFI wants to become more than a content site.

#### Offer Themes
- EF coaching through a SPED lens
- OT-informed environmental scaffolding for executive function
- home-school translation for EF supports
- when EF coaching is appropriate vs when referral is needed
- interdisciplinary case interpretation for practitioners

#### Suggested Pricing
- `79–199` for workshops
- `295–795` for short intensives
- `1,200+` for premium cohort-style training if delivered live

## Recommended Pricing Logic

EFI should avoid pricing that feels random. A clearer ladder:

### Low Ticket
- `19–79`
- tools, templates, guides, mini kits

### Mid Ticket
- `149–495`
- review products, interpretation products, lighter consult offers

### Premium
- `495–1,500+`
- support blueprints, intensives, school/team consults

This creates a cleaner path:

`Free diagnostic -> low-ticket toolkit -> mid-ticket review/blueprint -> premium consult/training`

## Compensation Models

The safest structures are explicit and scoped.

### Option 1. Flat Fee Per Deliverable
Best for review, revision, or product contribution work.
- module/page review: `200–500`
- toolkit/product contribution: `500–1,500`

### Option 2. Revenue Share By Offer
Best for co-created offers with ongoing value.
- recommended range: `10–25%` of net revenue for that specific product

### Option 3. Advisor Retainer
Best for ongoing review and periodic planning.
- recommended range: `300–1,000+/month`

### Option 4. Hybrid
- small upfront consulting fee
- plus revenue share on co-created offers

This is often the cleanest model when the expert is helping both shape and legitimize a product.

## Build Now / Build Next / Nice Later

### Build Now

These are the highest-confidence, most saleable offers EFI can ship first.

1. **School-to-Home Translation Review**
- fastest to package
- clearest value proposition
- easiest bridge from free tools to paid offer

2. **Family EF Support Blueprint**
- strongest flagship service
- best use of interdisciplinary review
- good mid-ticket anchor

3. **Small Toolkit Line**
- easiest way to create lower-friction paid entry points
- useful for impulse-buy behavior and email monetization

### Build Next

These should come after the first offers are selling and the site copy is adjusted.

1. **Classroom EF Systems Audit**
- good for educator-facing authority
- good bridge into school/team sales

2. **OT / SPED-informed workshop**
- strong authority product
- useful once EFI has clearer practitioner demand

3. **Expanded practitioner bundles**
- implementation templates
- school/home support packs
- premium downloadable resources

### Nice Later

These are worth considering, but they should not come before the first ladder works.

1. **Higher-touch EF intensives**
- more operationally demanding
- require tighter onboarding and capacity planning

2. **District / school packages**
- high upside
- longer sales cycle
- better once credibility assets and case examples are stronger

3. **Premium cohort or certificate-style interdisciplinary training**
- could become valuable later
- should follow clearer demand and stronger expert alignment

The strongest near-term bet is not trying to build a huge menu.

It is:
- one flagship family support offer
- one lighter translation/review offer
- one educator audit offer

That gives EFI:
