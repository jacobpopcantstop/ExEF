# EFI Business Foundation Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-19  
**Purpose:** Give EFI a practical checklist for business formation, legal/ops setup, financial separation, and a possible alumni-network background-check policy.

---

## Why This Matters Now

EFI is no longer just a website project. It now has:
- paid offers
- collaborators/advisors
- live checkout and fulfillment paths
- credential/review components
- a potential alumni and directory layer

That means the business side needs structure before operations get messier.

The right goal is not "become a giant company overnight."  
The goal is:
- separate business from personal finances
- reduce legal ambiguity
- make collaborator relationships cleaner
- make future directory/community policies defensible

---

## Best Immediate Priorities

### 1. Form the Business Entity

For EFI’s current stage, the most likely practical choice is an LLC.

Why:
- separates business operations from you personally better than operating informally
- cleaner for contracts, Stripe, bank accounts, and advisors
- more credible for future partnerships

Questions to answer:
- what state will the LLC be formed in?
- who owns it?
- will EFI stay single-member for now, or is there any shared ownership planned?

Immediate outputs:
- LLC formed
- EIN obtained
- operating agreement in place

---

### 2. Open a Business Bank Account

Do this as soon as the LLC and EIN are in place.

Why:
- separates personal and business spending
- makes bookkeeping and taxes much easier
- gives cleaner audit trails for Stripe revenue and collaborator payments

Immediate outputs:
- business checking account
- business savings/tax holding account if possible
- debit card dedicated to EFI

---

### 3. Set Up Basic Bookkeeping

You do not need a complicated finance stack yet, but you do need clean records.

Immediate needs:
- income tracking
- expense tracking
- contractor/advisor payment tracking
- category structure for taxes

Simple setup is fine:
- QuickBooks, Wave, Xero, or similar
- monthly reconciliation habit

At minimum, track:
- software/subscriptions
- contractors/advisors
- marketing
- hosting/infrastructure
- payment processing fees
- legal/professional fees

---

### 4. Clarify Legal Documents and Policies

The site already has some policy surfaces, but they now need to be treated as business-critical.

Must-have business-side legal surfaces:
- Terms
- Privacy Policy
- Refund / review / resubmission policy
- Scope-of-practice disclaimer
- Payment fulfillment language
- Contractor/advisor agreements

Likely near-term additions:
- advisor agreement
- contractor agreement
- rev-share addendum if you co-create products
- directory/alumni policy

---

### 5. Decide the Collaborator Model

Before EFI adds more people, decide whether experts are:
- advisors
- contractors
- co-creators
- rev-share partners

Do not leave this vague.

For each person, define:
- role
- deliverables
- compensation
- whether their name can be used publicly
- whether they own any IP they create
- whether EFI owns the final product

## Background Checks for an Alumni Network

Yes, EFI can require a background check for access to a private alumni directory, mentor status, or certain network privileges.

But it should be framed carefully.

Best use cases:
- alumni directory listing
- “verified alumni” badge
- mentor/community lead eligibility
- referral network participation

Avoid overstating it as:
- a clinical clearance
- a legal endorsement
- proof someone is safe in every setting

Better framing:
- `EFI Alumni Directory eligibility`
- `Background-screened directory status`
- `Verified directory participation`

---

## Recommended Background-Check Policy Structure

### Option A: Make It Required Only for Directory Participation

This is the safest and most practical first version.

Meaning:
- anyone can complete EFI training
- only screened participants can appear in a public or semi-public directory

Why this is smart:
- lowers legal and operational complexity
- avoids turning the whole certification into a gatekeeping system
- creates a cleaner “optional verified” layer

### Option B: Make It Required for Alumni Community Access

This is more aggressive and usually less necessary.

Problems:
- more privacy/admin burden
- higher risk of confusion and pushback
- not all community participation may warrant that level of screening

### Recommendation

Start with:
- optional alumni network overall
- required background check only for directory/referral/mentor status

---

## What the Policy Would Need

If EFI uses a real background check vendor, you need:
- explicit consent
- clear eligibility criteria
- privacy/disclosure language
- retention rules
- appeals/review process
- clear explanation of what the screening does and does not mean

Policy should answer:
- who gets screened
- what role screening applies to
- who pays for it
- how often it must be renewed
- what categories disqualify someone, if any
- who reviews results
- what happens if a person disputes the result

Important note:
- if a third-party screening report is used to deny someone access, there may be consumer-reporting and employment-adjacent legal issues depending on how this is structured
- EFI should get real legal review before making this an operational policy

## Best Practical Directory Model

If EFI wants a directory, the cleanest ladder is:

### Level 1. Alumni
- completed EFI training/review requirements
- private alumni community access if offered

### Level 2. Verified Alumni
- identity/contact confirmed
- agreement to directory terms
- maybe basic professional profile review

### Level 3. Screened Directory Status
- background check completed
- eligible for public listing / referrals / mentor status

That is much cleaner than trying to make one binary “certified or not” system do everything.

## Business Questions EFI Needs Answered Soon

1. Who legally owns EFI?
2. What exact entity name will contracts and Stripe accounts use?
3. What state is the business formed in?
4. Will EFI use contractors, advisors, or rev-share partners?
5. Who reviews legal/policy language before launch?
6. Will EFI offer refunds? Under what conditions?
7. What counts as product fulfillment for each paid offer?
8. Will alumni directory participation be optional or built into certification?
9. Will EFI eventually carry insurance for coaching/training/advisory activity?
10. What exact claims should EFI avoid making publicly?

## What To Do On The Site

Once the business side is more formal, the site should reflect it in small but important ways:

### Update Legal Pages
- make sure entity name is correct
- add principal office/jurisdiction if needed
- align contact info

### Update Store Language
- make fulfillment timelines explicit
- state refund/resubmission policy clearly
- state what is reviewed by a human

### Update About / Advisor Pages
- use precise role titles
- avoid vague endorsement language

### If a Directory Is Added
- create a dedicated page explaining:
- who is listed
- what screening means
- what it does not mean
- how to report concerns

Do not try to solve every business/legal question at once.

The right first move is:
- create the LLC
- get the bank account
- separate finances
- define collaborator agreements

That gets EFI from “project with revenue” to “actual business with operational boundaries.”
