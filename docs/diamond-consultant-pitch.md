# An Invitation to Partner with EFI
**For Diamond — a conversation, not a contract**

---

## What This Is

This isn't a job offer or a formal proposal. It's an invitation to think together about whether what I'm building at EFI is something you'd want to be part of — on your terms, in your transition.

I've watched how you work. The way you read an IEP not just for what's in it but for what's missing. The way you translate between what a family is experiencing at home and what a school team can actually act on. That skill is rare, and it doesn't retire when you do.

EFI is building the infrastructure to put that kind of expertise where families can reach it. I'd like to talk about whether you want to be part of that.

---

## What EFI Is

The Executive Functioning Institute is an open-curriculum coaching and credentialing program built around the research of Barkley, Brown, Dawson & Guare, and Ward. The public side is free — an open curriculum, assessment tools, and planning resources anyone can use. The paid side is reviewer-led: coaching, certification review, and (coming soon) specialist referrals.

The site already has a public certification pathway for EF coaches. The next layer — the one I'm building now — is a specialist services directory: connecting families with the right person depending on whether their problem is a coaching problem, a school-system problem, or a sensory/motor problem.

Educational specialist services is one of those three lanes. It's currently listed as "coming soon." You're the first person I've thought of to make it real.

## The Three Things I'm Asking You to Consider

### 1. List yourself as an Educational Specialist Consultant

Your name, credentials, and a brief description of what you do — on the EFI Services page. Families who land there looking for school-system navigation would see you as the resource. This is a referral listing, not a job. You control your availability, your rates, and who you take on. EFI provides the surface; you provide the expertise.

What it looks like for you: a short bio and a contact path. Families reach out to you directly. No middleman, no platform fee.

### 2. Serve as a Certification Reviewer for the CEFC Credential

The EFI Certified Executive Function Coach credential requires a human reviewer — someone who can read a capstone submission, evaluate it against the competency rubric, and sign off on whether the candidate is ready to practice.

Right now I'm the only reviewer. That's a bottleneck and a single point of failure. Having a credentialed SPED professional as a second reviewer does two things: it adds legitimacy to the credential and it distributes the review load as enrollment grows.

What it looks like for you: reviewing capstone submissions on a flexible schedule — likely a few per month to start. Paid per review. You'd be evaluating whether a coaching candidate demonstrates the competencies we've defined, not teaching new material.

### 3. Provide IEP and Educator Services to EFI Families

This is the biggest ask and the most open-ended one, so I want to describe it carefully.

Some families who come to EFI have a coaching-shaped problem. Others have a school-system-shaped problem: IEPs that don't reflect what the student actually needs, placement decisions being made without the right evidence, teams that aren't communicating, or families who don't know what they're entitled to ask for.

Those families need someone who knows how to read a room at a school meeting. Someone who can look at a present-levels statement and spot the gap between what the school is measuring and what the kid is struggling with. Someone who's been on the other side of the table and knows what moves the needle.

That's you.

What I'm imagining: you take a small number of family consultation cases — by referral from EFI, on your own schedule, at your own rate. I provide the intake routing. You provide the expertise. This could be as light as a single-session IEP review, or as involved as ongoing advocacy support through a placement process.

This isn't a commitment. It's a door. We'd figure out the right shape together.

## What You'd Get Out of It

**A flexible post-retirement practice on your terms.** You're not starting a business from scratch — the platform, the audience, and the infrastructure already exist. You're stepping into a lane that's built for you.

**A credentialed context for your expertise.** Your work would be framed within a curriculum and competency system that treats EF seriously. Not just "consulting" — structured, documented, recognized.

**Ongoing connection to the field you know.** If leaving the classroom means losing the professional community and the sense of doing something that matters — this keeps you in it without the institutional weight.

**Compensation that reflects your expertise.** Certification reviews would be paid per submission. Family consulting would be at your rate. Listing on the site is free to you.

This is as lightweight or as substantial as you want it to be.
