Field Notes

What the research actually says

Developmental Timeline

EF maturation can lag by about 30%

For many ADHD adults, regulation systems develop on a different schedule. That reframes "late bloomer" histories, career timing, and self-comparison.

Barkley; Shaw, NIH

State-Dependence

Performance shifts with interest as much as effort

The same person can hyperfocus on a hobby and stall on a chore. EF expression is highly context-dependent — which is why mapping context matters.

Thomas E. Brown

Activation

Interest, novelty, and urgency act like fuel

The ADHD brain often needs one of those signals to generate enough activation for sustained focus. Good coaching builds those levers into the task itself.

Volkow; Dodson

Working Memory

Interruptions can break the whole sequence

Working memory is especially vulnerable in ADHD. Protecting the environment and offloading steps visually beats trying to remember harder.

Thomas E. Brown

Emotional Regulation

Emotional control sits in the core symptom picture

Overwhelm, rejection sensitivity, and rapid escalation are performance issues in their own right. Time management work without regulation collapses under pressure.

Russell Barkley

Delay Aversion

Far-off rewards weaken follow-through

If the payoff is too distant, the task feels neurologically cold. Immediate feedback and visible progress land much more reliably.

Sonuga-Barke

Movement

Fidgeting can support thinking

Respectful movement is often compensatory. When people can move, they hold working memory and regulation more effectively.

Rapport; Rotz & Wright

Exercise

Thirty minutes can change the whole day

Acute movement improves inhibitory control and working memory quickly enough to matter for the rest of the day. Activation sometimes starts with physiology.

John Ratey

External Scaffolds

Timers and body doubling offload the brain

These supports shift initiation and follow-through into the environment — more reliable than willpower-heavy systems during overload.

Ramsay & Rostain

Shame Reduction

Language changes outcomes

When chronic disorganization is understood as a neurological pattern, people stop burning energy on shame and start building systems that actually work.

Hallowell & Ratey

What is Executive Function Coaching?

Executive function coaching helps you build practical systems for planning, time management, task initiation, and follow-through. It draws from neuroscience, point-of-performance design, and environmental scaffolding.
