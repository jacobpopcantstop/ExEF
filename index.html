Does this sound familiar?

You know what you need to do.
You just can't seem to start.

Executive dysfunction isn't a character flaw or a lack of willpower. It's a real, well-documented difference in how the brain manages planning, attention, time, and follow-through — and it shows up differently in every person who lives with it.

Time seems to disappear

Tasks that should take minutes stretch into hours — or never start at all. Time blindness is one of the most common and least-understood EF challenges.

Plans fall apart in execution

You can visualize a goal perfectly and still not take the first step. The gap between intention and action is a hallmark of dysregulated executive function.

It affects every area of life

Home, school, work, and relationships all feel the pressure. EF dysfunction doesn't stay in one lane — and neither should the support around it.

What we do about it

Science-grounded tools and coaching for real EF challenges

The Executive Functioning Institute brings together open curriculum, evidence-based assessments, and reviewed coaching built on the frameworks of Barkley, Brown, Dawson & Guare, and Ward. Whether you are a parent trying to understand a child, an educator building support structures, or a professional deepening your practice — there is a clear route here for you.

Reviewed Boundary

Where paid review actually begins

EFI does not charge for browsing or basic study. The paid layer begins only when accountable human labor and credential operations start.

Visitors can inspect curriculum pages, tools, source notes, and standards without paying for access.

Submit Work for Human Review

Payment starts when EFI is asked to score assignments, interpret results, track readiness, or give written evaluator feedback.

Move Into Capstone Judgment

The most labor-intensive layer is capstone review: rubric scoring, revision notes, re-review support, and credential decisions.

Use Store Only After Fit Is Clear

The store should confirm that route, not explain it. Buyers should already know whether they need interpretation, enrollment, or final review.
